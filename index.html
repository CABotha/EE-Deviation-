Employment Equity Deviation Record

Purpose:

The purpose of this tool is to document relevant information and evidence in the situation where an appointment (recruitment or promotion) is made that is not in alignment with the prioritised EE Plan and its accompanying Targets. Once completed, the information captured in this tool can be exported and saved in order to create a portfolio that could be used in justifying deviations from the 7 justifiable reasons contained in the EE Regulations. It may be useful as evidence during DG Reviews and litigation as well as in providing feedback to EE Committees and other stakeholders.

Procedure:

Prior to making an offer for employment and/or promotion where the preferred candidate is not the prioritised candidate based on the EE analysis and plan, complete the prompts and/or questions below and add in relevant supporting information. It would be useful to use AI in a secure environment to assist in determining whether a candidate(s) is "suitably qualified" as envisaged in s20(3)-(5). This will provide data-driven insights into whether the said candidate could acquire the required competencies in a reasonable time.

Assessing whether prioritised designated persons under the EE plan and targets, who applied for the position did not meet with the requirements of the definition of Suitably Qualified Person (SQP)

The EE Act requires employers to satisfy themselves that designated persons who apply for positions are not suitably qualified, before appointing an individual who is not aligned with the EE targets. Herewith are the provisions of s20(3)-(5). Importantly, one of the most important justifications will be linked to proving that it would take unreasonably long for the candidate to meet the required competencies.

20(3) For purposes of this Act, a person may be suitably qualified for a job as a result of any one of, or any combination of that person's –

(a) formal qualifications;
(b) prior learning;
(c) relevant experience; or
(d) capacity to acquire, within a reasonable time, the ability to do the job.

(4) When determining whether a person is suitably qualified for a job, an employer must–

(a) review all the factors listed in subsection (3); and
(b) determine whether that person has the ability to do the job in terms of any one of, or any combination of those factors.

(5) In making a determination under subsection (4), an employer may not unfairly discriminate against a person solely on the grounds of that person's lack of relevant experience.
